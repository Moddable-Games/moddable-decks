▲ Our mission 02 / 20

Publish moddable tabletop games — and turn players, artists and designers into co-creators.

An open-source ethos.

Every rulebook is forkable. Every component is replaceable. Every variant is a pull request waiting to happen. Modders aren't a side channel — they're how the catalogue grows.

Why we're called Moddable.

Not a publisher. Not a platform. A toolkit — a hex-grid engine, a print-on-demand pipeline, and a marketplace — that turns the boards on your shelf into the boards you wanted them to be.

▲ Why hexagons 04 / 20

Replacing
squares with
hexagons.

Six neighbours instead of four. No diagonal exceptions. No edge-case rules. The math of strategy gets cleaner the moment the grid does.

6 adjacencies 0 diagonals ∞ variants
SQUARES · 4 NEIGHBOURS
HEXAGONS · 6 NEIGHBOURS

Why now: we're building our own online engine for hosting hex-based games, so any tabletop designer can ship a hex variant without rolling their own infrastructure.

How we make money
without fearing the copycats.

Two tracks, run in parallel. One bootstraps slowly with revenue. The other unlocks with funding — and shortens the timeline.

TRACK A Bootstrap · slow

Organic growth.

Revenue funds the next print run. No outside cheque required.

  • 01Print-on-demand for first runs — no inventory risk
  • 02Online store for generic components — hexes, tokens, dice
  • 03Branded merchandise and tabletop tools
  • 04Licensing agreements with adjacent IP holders
  • 05Original games commissioned for specific brands
TRACK B Funded · fast

Engineered growth.

Outside capital builds the engine, the marketplace, and the team.

  • 01Build the online engine for hex-based games at scale
  • 02Self-publishing tools for designers — drag-and-drop rulebooks
  • 03Marketplace for games, expansions and printable components
  • 04Collect training data for stronger in-game bots
